Executive Summary

In this case study, we explore the development and implementation of a machine learning model to predict stroke risk among patients using various health metrics. This initiative addresses the critical need for early identification of high-risk individuals, enabling timely intervention and personalized treatment plans. By leveraging advanced algorithms and comprehensive patient data, we demonstrate how predictive analytics can enhance preventive healthcare measures and improve patient outcomes.


Background

Stroke is a major global health concern, causing significant mortality and long-term disability. Accurate stroke risk prediction can enable early interventions, reducing the incidence and severity of stroke events. Advances in machine learning and data analytics offer new opportunities to utilize extensive patient data to create predictive models.



Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to develop and implement a predictive model that accurately forecasts stroke risk using comprehensive patient data.


Problem Statement

Strokes are a leading cause of mortality and serious long-term disability, affecting approximately 15 million people worldwide each year. From 2011 to 2021, the age-adjusted stroke death rate in the United States increased by 8.4%, while the actual number of stroke deaths rose by 26.3%.
