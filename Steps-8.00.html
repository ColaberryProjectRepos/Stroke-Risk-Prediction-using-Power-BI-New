Visualization: Case 2 - Comprehensive Health Monitoring for High-Risk Seniors

Positive Insight: Seniors (55-70 years) with a stroke occurrence rate of 8% have significant risk factors, including hypertension at 16% and heart disease at 9%.


Negative Insight: Despite these high-risk factors, comprehensive health monitoring is often underutilized, leading to higher stroke occurrences and healthcare costs.



Chart: Crude stroke mortality rates among older US adults (=65 years of age), 2008 to 2019.

Source: Unpublished National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute tabulation using Centers for Disease Control and Prevention Wide-Ranging Online Data for Epidemiological Research.

 

ROI: Implementing a comprehensive health monitoring system that includes regular check-ups and personalized health plans can reduce stroke occurrence by 30%, resulting in annual savings of $2,500,000. The ROI percentage for this program is projected at 450%.
